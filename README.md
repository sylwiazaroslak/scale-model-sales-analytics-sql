# 🏎️ Scale Model Cars Sales Performance & Inventory Optimization (SQL)

## Project Overview
In global retail, transforming transactional data into structured, strategic intelligence is vital for sustaining profit margins and optimizing inventory. This data analytics project interrogates a relational sales database tracking a global scale-model vehicle company. 

The primary business directive is to process **8 interconnected tables containing over 3,200 records** to answer two critical executive questions:
1. **Inventory Management:** Which high-demand products face severe stockouts, and where should reordering capital be deployed?
2. **Customer Segmentation:** Which regional consumer profiles and elite tier accounts generate the company’s core profitability?

---

## 🔎 Executive Summary & Key Insights
* **The 80/20 Profit Pareto Distribution:** Financial profiling revealed an aggressive concentration of wealth among a tiny subset of elite accounts. The top two corporate clients, **Euro+ Shopping Channel (Madrid, Spain)** contributing **$326,519.66** and **Mini Gifts Distributors Ltd. (San Rafael, USA)** contributing **$236,769.39**, generate a massive, disproportionate share of total operational profit.
* **Geographical Hub Clustering:** High-margin revenue is tightly localized in specific global micro-clusters. While the United States retains a dominant market footprint (San Rafael, NYC, San Jose, San Francisco), European cities such as **Madrid, Spain** and **Nantes/Paris, France** punch significantly above their weight in margin value.
* **The High-Velocity Inventory Trap:** Multiple scale-model SKUs exhibit highly accelerated sales rates relative to their current warehouse stock capacity. This operational mismatch indicates that consumer demand is outpacing supply chain speeds, leading to stockouts and lost revenue on core "cash cow" products.
